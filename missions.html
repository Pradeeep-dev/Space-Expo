Space Missions

Eight landmark missions that changed how we see the universe.

01
NASA · Crewed Lunar

Apollo 11

Jul 1969Launch
MoonDestination
8 daysDuration

"One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." Apollo 11 made history as the first crewed mission to land on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

👨‍🚀 The guidance computer had less power than a modern pocket calculator.
✓ Success
02
NASA · Interstellar

Voyager 1

Sep 1977Launch
InterstellarDestination
46+ yrsActive

The farthest human-made object in existence. Voyager 1 crossed into interstellar space in 2012 and still transmits data from over 24 billion kilometres away — using just 22.4 watts of power.

📡 Signals take 22+ hours to reach Earth at the speed of light.
⬤ Active
03
NASA/ESA · Observatory

Hubble

Apr 1990Launch
Low OrbitAltitude
35+ yrsActive

The telescope that changed how we see the universe. Over 1.3 million observations — including the iconic "Pillars of Creation" and the Ultra Deep Field, revealing 10,000 galaxies in one tiny patch of sky.

🔭 Orbits Earth every 95 minutes at 27,300 km/h.
⬤ Active
04
NASA/ESA · Saturn Orbiter

Cassini

Oct 1997Launch
SaturnDestination
20 yearsDuration

Cassini spent 13 years orbiting Saturn, revealing its rings, moons, and storms in extraordinary detail. The Huygens probe landed on Titan — the first landing in the outer Solar System. In 2017 it made its dramatic final plunge into Saturn.

🪐 Discovered that Enceladus sprays liquid water geysers into space.
● Complete
05
NASA/ESA/CSA · Deep Space

James Webb

Dec 2021Launch
L2 PointLocation
4+ yrsActive

Hubble's successor. JWST peers through dust clouds to see the first galaxies formed after the Big Bang — 13.5 billion years ago. Its gold-coated hexagonal mirror spans 6.5 metres and its 2022 images stunned the world.

🌌 Can detect a bumblebee at the Moon's distance by its heat alone.
⬤ Active
06
NASA · Planetary Defence

DART

Nov 2021Launch
DimorphosTarget
6.6 km/sImpact Speed

The world's first planetary defence test. NASA's spacecraft deliberately crashed into asteroid Dimorphos in September 2022, successfully changing its orbit by 32 minutes — proving humanity can deflect a dangerous asteroid.

☄️ Impact ejected 1,000 tonnes of rock, creating a 10,000 km debris tail.
✓ Success
07
ISRO · Lunar Lander

Chandrayaan-3

Jul 2023Launch
South PoleLanding Site
40 daysJourney

India's crowning space achievement. On 23 August 2023, Vikram lander touched down near the lunar south pole — making India the first nation to achieve this. The Pragyan rover confirmed sulphur and other elements on the surface.

🇮🇳 India became the 4th country to land on the Moon and 1st at the south pole.
✓ Success
08
ISRO · Solar Observatory

Aditya-L1

Sep 2023Launch
Sun-L1Destination
5 yearsMission Life

India's first dedicated solar mission. Placed in a halo orbit around the Sun-Earth Lagrange Point 1, it studies the solar corona, solar winds, and space weather — data that helps predict geomagnetic storms affecting Earth.

☀️ Orbits 1.5 million km from Earth — an unobstructed view of the Sun forever.
⬤ Active
